Setting up your GLM

If you know how to run your standard mass-univariate GLM, then this step is going to be a breeze! Here, I assume you have finished preprocessing your data and prepared it for the GLM step. You need the following information:

  • Onsets: To be organized in a .mat file in a single vector such that every trial appears in its own column. See example here: XXXX. The duration of your trials (e.g., boxcar or delta) depends on your specific experimental design. SPM also requires column names, these are arbitrary (I do trial_0001 ... trial_N)
  • Regressors: Your 6 realignment parameters concatenated into a single session and saved into a .txt file. DO NOT include spike regressors!! The inclusion of spike regressors leads to highly outlying parameter estimates.
  • Scans: Preprocessed fMRI data in a .nii format. The fNIM lab has typically used unsmoothed data, however, we have recently made the switch to smoothed data as we dropped to 2 mm isotropic voxels

Once you specify your GLM SPM.mat, you will want to apply spm_fmri_concatenate() to model session means. Only then you can estimate your model. This is the same approach as the standard GLM!
